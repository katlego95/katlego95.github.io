Springboks brought to their knees by England on Outbound Tour.

It wasn’t a great day at the office for the Springboks as they suffered a 37 – 12 defeat against England at Twickenham Stadium on Saturday, 12th of November. South Africa’s loss to the English ends a decade-long winning streak.

Unfavourable weather conditions aside, the men in green and gold started strongly but ultimately were no match for Eddie Jones’ team who proceeded to win their 10th consecutive game of the season.
Ill-discipline by England saw South Africa take an early 6 – 0 lead through a penalty and drop goal by Pat Lambie. England retaliated with the first try of the game courtesy of Jonny May and Owen Farrell’s conversion. Lambie missed a penalty but shortly rectified this error by converting an offside penalty to reclaim the lead 9 – 7. In a tough contest, Eben Etzebeth was sent off after being involved in a head to head collision with Billy Vunipola.
Shortly after, England conceded a penalty converted by Farrell to take the lead once again, there was no stopping the men in white as they continued to put more points on the board with a Courtney Lawes try subsequently converted by Farrell. England were awarded a try before the break, Elliot Daly’s long range kick gave them 20 – 9 lead.
South Africa’s hopes for an early comeback in the second half were diminished when George Ford added another try, which Farrell converted to make the score 27 – 9. Lady luck was not on the Springboks’ side as they were penalized for an offside and Farrell’s conversion increased the score once again.
On the hour mark, substitute Johan Goosen went over the touch line for SA’s first try of the match. England hit back with a try from the kicker himself, Farrell, which he converted to stretch England’s’ lead to 37 – 14. Willie Le Roux scored a consolation try in the final minute of the game, which was converted by Ruan Combrink.
The Springbok’s points were largely attained via England’s errors. Their overall performance uninspiring and they never seemed in control of the game. I never saw anything spectacular in England’s performance, except for rectifying their discipline and keeping to the basics. Had the Springboks done the same, we could have definitely put up a good fight.
South Africa take on Italy at Stadio Artemio Franchi, Florence, Italy on the 19th of November. This is presumably one of the “easier” teams they come against on the Castle Year-End Tour. Apart from the addition of veteran Bryan Habana and Nizzam Carr in the starting 15 and three changes to the bench, the rest of the team will be kept unchanged. The game is definitely an opportunity for the team to secure their first win of the tour.Kickoff at 4pm CAT.
Team: Willie le Roux, Ruan Combrinck, Francois Venter, Damian de Allende, Bryan Habana, Pat Lambie, Rudy Paige, Warren Whiteley, Willem Alberts, Nizaam Carr, Lood de Jager, Pieter-Steph du Toit, Vincent Koch, Adriaan Strauss (captain), Tendai Mtawarira
Substitutes: Bongi Mbonambi, Steven Kitshoff, Trevor Nyakane, Franco Mostert, Oupa Mohoje, Faf de Klerk, Elton Jantjies, Johan Goosen
