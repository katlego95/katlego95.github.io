Big boy pants for Kolisi.

Loose forward Siya Kolisi has been named as the club captain for the DHL Stormers ahead of the 2017 Super Rugby tournament which kicks off on the 23rd of February. With 76 caps for the Stormers franchise, he and vice-captain Eben Etzebeth will lead the team in the upcoming competition.

The captaincy duo started their ranks within the Western Province Rugby Institute in 2010 along with team mates Frans Malherbe, Nizaam Carr and ‘Scarra’ Ntubeni. And both proceeded to join the Stomers in 2012, played alongside each other for five seasons and are reportedly elated to share the new journey together. Stormers have done away with the appointment of the co-captains, a responsibility which saw Juan de Jongh and Frans Malherbe in charge last year. Although fairly young, Kolisi has shown tremendous growth both on and off the field and is a definite favourite amongst team management, players and fans. With a number of strong leaders in the squad, the newly appointed captain will be competently supported by core senior players.
The 25-year old is singled out for his level-headed approach to things and is undoubtedly one of the most respected members of the team. It is also worth noting that Kolisi experienced a career slump which served as an opportunity to turn his life around. Siya hit back with enhanced rugby talent and took on a leadership role in his personal life. He adopted his brother and sister, became a father and married his long-time girlfriend, Rachel Smith, last year August. His journey and growth has definitely prepared him for the responsibility coach Robbie Fleck has bestowed on him.
Kolisi will take charge of his first game at Newlands on February 25 when the Stomers kick-off their Super Rugby campaign against the Blue Bulls. It won’t be an easy task for the newly appointed Kolisi as they face five New Zealand teams in the new format of the competition.
I am happy at the announcement. He is deserving and this accolade shall do wonders for his career, but also tip him for future Springbok captain selection. I have no doubt that Kolisi will be an excellent captain and is ready to lead the team in the upcoming season. The Stormers players and supporters rally behind the appointment and I hope he enjoys the journey of coin tosses and cutting half time orange wedges that lie ahead of him.
